About Me

My name is Don. I am a developer and a student of the Computer & Electrical Engineering Department of the University of Houston. I also am an undergraduate researcher and the current president of the college Tau Beta Pi chapter. At 21 years of age, I have been lucky enough to be exposed to many topics that I have fallen in love with: computer architecture & digital design, programming & development, digital signal & systems analysis, numerical methods & data analysis and even modern physics just to name a few. I am facinated at how the natural sciences have come together to form the technological infrastructure that is now the backbone of today's society. Quite frankly, I love to learn whatever I can.

I have a solid background in programming from low-level languages (x86, MIPS, Freescale) to high-level languages (C/C++/C#, MATLAB, Python, SQL) along with HDLs on FPGAs and some web development. I have a strong mathematical foundation from my engineering background which I believe helps me tremendously with critical problem solving and algorithm development. I also have experience with MSSQL and Oracle DBs and Powershell scripting.

My ultimate goal is to use my skill set to assist in what I see as a problem in social-economic immobility in our society. I believe in the cliche that knowledge is power and I would love to use my background to help others, especially those who have less opportunity due to their personal situations, to learn and advance themselves so that they may have a chance to succeed.
